Marcus Aurelius

Roman Emperor and Stoic Philosopher

Biography

Marcus Aurelius was Roman emperor from 161 to 180 and a Stoic philosopher. He was the last of the rulers known as the Five Good Emperors and the last emperor of the Pax Romana, an age of relative peace and stability for the Roman Empire. He is best known for his philosophical work "Meditations," written in Greek while on campaign between 170 and 180.

Philosophy

As a practitioner of Stoicism, Marcus Aurelius believed in the development of self-control and fortitude as a means to overcome destructive emotions. His personal writings on Stoic philosophy, now commonly known as "Meditations," are still revered as a literary monument to a government of service and duty.

Quotes

"The happiness of your life depends upon the quality of your thoughts."
"You have power over your mind - not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength."
"The best revenge is to be unlike him who performed the injury."
